# Group 2: Math and Numbers - Dataset Generation Prompt

## Overview
Generate 600,000 samples for basic math and number skills training. This is a foundational group with high priority in curriculum learning.

## Output Format
Simple key-value JSON format in `curriculum_training_data/group2.json`:
```json
{
  "Can you count till 5?": "1, 2, 3, 4, 5",
  "What comes after 46?": "47",
  "What is 2 + 2?": "4",
  ...
}
```

---

## Statement Breakdown (6 Statement Types)

### Statement 1: Counting Sequences (Till 100)
**Example**: {Can you count till 5?: "1, 2, 3, 4, 5"}

**Prompt**: Create data points for counting sequences up to 100. Try different combinations of:

(i) **Semantic variations**:
- "Can you count till X?"
- "Count from 1 to X"
- "What are the numbers from 1 to X?"
- "List the numbers up to X"
- "Count to X for me"
- "Show me counting till X"
- "What numbers come when counting to X?"
- "Give me the count up to X"
- "Recite numbers from 1 to X"
- "Count the numbers till X"
- "What is the sequence from 1 to X?"
- "List numbers counting to X"

(ii) **Target numbers**: Use numbers from 1 to 100
- Small counts: 1-10 (frequent)
- Medium counts: 11-30 (moderate)
- Large counts: 31-100 (less frequent but covered)

(iii) **Answer format**: Comma-separated sequence (e.g., "1, 2, 3, 4, 5")

**Difficulty mix**: 40% easy (count to 1-10), 40% medium (count to 11-30), 20% hard (count to 31-100)

---

### Statement 2: Before and After Queries
**Example**: {What comes after 46?: "47"}, {What comes before 50?: "49"}

**Prompt**: Create data points for number sequence understanding (before/after). Try different combinations of:

(i) **Semantic variations for "After"**:
- "What comes after X?"
- "What is the number after X?"
- "Which number follows X?"
- "What number is next after X?"
- "Tell me what comes after X"
- "Give me the number that follows X"
- "What is X plus one?"
- "Name the number after X"
- "What succeeds X?"
- "Which number comes right after X?"

(ii) **Semantic variations for "Before"**:
- "What comes before X?"
- "What is the number before X?"
- "Which number precedes X?"
- "What number is just before X?"
- "Tell me what comes before X"
- "Give me the number that precedes X"
- "What is X minus one?"
- "Name the number before X"
- "What precedes X?"
- "Which number comes right before X?"

(iii) **Number range**: 
- Positive numbers: 1-1000
- Zero: Include edge cases like "What comes after 0?" (answer: 1)
- Negative numbers: -100 to 0 (e.g., "What comes after -5?": "-4")

(iv) **Answer format**: Single number (e.g., "47", "-4", "100")

**Difficulty mix**: 40% easy (1-100), 40% medium (101-500, includes negatives), 20% hard (501-1000, negative ranges)

### Statement 3: Word Problems with Objects
**Example**: {If you have one apple, and get one more, how many apples now?: "2"}

**Prompt**: Create data points for word problems involving objects and arithmetic reasoning. Try different combinations of:

(i) **Semantic variations**:
- "If you have X [objects], and get Y more, how many [objects] now?"
- "You have X [objects]. If you get Y more, how many do you have?"
- "There are X [objects]. If Y more are added, what is the total?"
- "Start with X [objects], add Y more. How many in all?"
- "X [objects] plus Y more [objects] equals how many?"
- "If I give you Y [objects] and you already have X, how many total?"
- "You had X [objects], then found Y more. How many [objects] altogether?"
- "Combine X [objects] with Y [objects]. What do you get?"

(ii) **Subtraction variations**:
- "If you have X [objects], and give away Y, how many left?"
- "You have X [objects]. If Y are taken away, how many remain?"
- "There are X [objects]. If you lose Y, how many are left?"
- "Start with X [objects], remove Y. How many now?"
- "X [objects] minus Y equals how many?"
- "If Y [objects] are eaten from X, how many left?"

(iii) **Mixed operations**:
- "You have X [objects], get Y more, then lose Z. How many now?"
- "Start with X, add Y, subtract Z. What's the answer?"

(iv) **Objects to use**: 
- Fruits: apples, oranges, bananas, mangoes, grapes, strawberries, cherries
- Animals: cats, dogs, birds, fish, rabbits, ducks, chickens
- Toys: balls, dolls, cars, blocks, puzzles, crayons, marbles
- Food: cookies, candies, chocolates, pizzas, cupcakes, sandwiches
- School items: pencils, books, erasers, notebooks, pens, rulers
- Nature: flowers, leaves, stones, shells, butterflies, stars

(v) **Number combinations**:
- Single digits: 1-9 combined with 1-9
- Two digit with single: 10-50 combined with 1-9
- Numbers can be written as words or digits: "one" or "1", "two" or "2"

(vi) **Answer format**: Single number (e.g., "2", "15", "8")

**Difficulty mix**: 40% easy (single digit + single digit), 40% medium (two operations or larger numbers), 20% hard (three operations, larger numbers)

### Statement 4: Number Comparisons (Including Positive and Negative)
**Example**: {Which number is greater, 5 or -9?: "5"}, {Which number is smaller, 100 or 99?: "99"}

**Prompt**: Create data points for comparing numbers including positive, negative, and zero. Try different combinations of:

(i) **Semantic variations for "Greater"**:
- "Which number is greater, X or Y?"
- "Which is bigger, X or Y?"
- "What is larger, X or Y?"
- "Between X and Y, which is greater?"
- "Compare X and Y. Which is bigger?"
- "Is X greater than Y? If so, say X, else say Y"
- "Pick the larger number: X or Y"
- "Tell me which is more, X or Y"
- "Which number is higher, X or Y?"
- "The bigger of X and Y is?"

(ii) **Semantic variations for "Smaller"**:
- "Which number is smaller, X or Y?"
- "Which is less, X or Y?"
- "What is smaller, X or Y?"
- "Between X and Y, which is smaller?"
- "Compare X and Y. Which is less?"
- "Pick the smaller number: X or Y"
- "Tell me which is less, X or Y"
- "Which number is lower, X or Y?"
- "The smaller of X and Y is?"

(iii) **Number combinations**:
- **Positive vs Positive**: 5 vs 10, 100 vs 99, 1000 vs 999
- **Negative vs Negative**: -5 vs -10 (answer: -5 is greater), -100 vs -50
- **Positive vs Negative**: 5 vs -9 (answer: 5 is greater), -7 vs 3
- **Zero comparisons**: 0 vs 5, 0 vs -5, 0 vs 0
- **Large numbers**: 1000 vs 999, 5000 vs 5001
- **Equal numbers**: 5 vs 5, -3 vs -3 (answer: "equal" or "both are equal")

(iv) **Number ranges**:
- Small: -100 to 100
- Medium: -1000 to 1000
- Large: -10000 to 10000

(v) **Answer format**: The correct number, or "equal"/"both are equal" for equal values

**Difficulty mix**: 
- 30% easy (positive vs positive, obvious differences)
- 40% medium (includes negatives, close numbers)
- 20% hard (negative vs negative, large numbers, tricky cases like -1 vs -2)
- 10% equal cases

### Statement 5: Direct Mathematical Queries (Up to 4 Terms)
**Example**: {What is 2 + 2?: "4"}, {What is 5 x 5?: "25"}, {What is 10 - 3 + 2?: "9"}

**Prompt**: Create data points for direct arithmetic queries. Go up to 4 term queries. Try different combinations of:

(i) **Semantic variations**:
- "What is X + Y?"
- "Calculate X - Y"
- "X times Y equals?"
- "What is X multiplied by Y?"
- "Compute X ÷ Y"
- "What is X divided by Y?"
- "X + Y = ?"
- "Solve: X - Y"
- "Find the value of X × Y"
- "What do you get when you add X and Y?"
- "What is the sum of X and Y?"
- "What is the difference between X and Y?"
- "What is the product of X and Y?"
- "What is the quotient of X and Y?"

(ii) **Operations**:
- **Addition (+)**: "What is 5 + 3?", "What is 12 + 8?"
- **Subtraction (-)**: "What is 10 - 4?", "What is 25 - 7?"
- **Multiplication (× or x or *)**: "What is 6 × 7?", "What is 5 x 5?", "What is 3 * 4?"
- **Division (÷ or /)**: "What is 20 ÷ 4?", "What is 15 / 3?"

(iii) **Number of terms**:
- **2 terms**: "What is 5 + 3?" → "8"
- **3 terms**: "What is 5 + 3 - 2?" → "6"
- **4 terms**: "What is 10 + 5 - 3 + 2?" → "14"

(iv) **Mixed operations (for 3-4 terms)**:
- "What is 5 + 3 × 2?" → "11" (order of operations)
- "What is 10 - 4 + 2?" → "8"
- "What is 6 × 2 ÷ 3?" → "4"
- "What is 2 + 3 × 4 - 1?" → "13"

(v) **Number ranges**:
- Addition/Subtraction: operands 0-100, results -100 to 200
- Multiplication: operands 0-12 (times tables), some larger (up to 20)
- Division: ensure clean division (no remainders for basic), divisors 1-12

(vi) **Special cases**:
- Zero operations: "What is 5 + 0?", "What is 0 × 7?"
- Identity: "What is 10 × 1?", "What is 15 ÷ 1?"
- Negative results: "What is 3 - 8?" → "-5"

(vii) **Answer format**: Single number (integer), can be negative

**Difficulty mix**:
- 30% easy (2 terms, single digits, basic operations)
- 40% medium (2-3 terms, two-digit numbers, mixed operations)
- 20% hard (3-4 terms, order of operations, larger numbers)
- 10% special cases (zeros, negatives)

### Statement 6: Word-Based Mathematical Queries
**Example**: {What is 4 less than 10?: "6"}, {What is 3 more than 7?: "10"}

**Prompt**: Create data points for word-based arithmetic. Answer should always be a number. Try different combinations of:

(i) **Semantic variations for Addition**:
- "What is X more than Y?" → Y + X
- "What is X added to Y?" → X + Y
- "What is the sum of X and Y?" → X + Y
- "What is X plus Y?" → X + Y
- "If you add X to Y, what do you get?" → X + Y
- "X increased by Y is?" → X + Y
- "What is Y with X added?" → Y + X

(ii) **Semantic variations for Subtraction**:
- "What is X less than Y?" → Y - X
- "What is Y minus X?" → Y - X
- "What is the difference between Y and X?" → Y - X (larger - smaller)
- "What is X subtracted from Y?" → Y - X
- "Y decreased by X is?" → Y - X
- "What remains when X is taken from Y?" → Y - X
- "X fewer than Y is?" → Y - X

(iii) **Semantic variations for Multiplication**:
- "What is X times Y?" → X × Y
- "What is double of X?" → X × 2
- "What is triple of X?" → X × 3
- "What is twice X?" → X × 2
- "What is thrice X?" → X × 3
- "What is X multiplied by Y?" → X × Y
- "What is half of X?" → X ÷ 2
- "What is a quarter of X?" → X ÷ 4

(iv) **Semantic variations for Division**:
- "What is X divided by Y?" → X ÷ Y
- "X split into Y parts gives?" → X ÷ Y
- "How many times does Y go into X?" → X ÷ Y
- "What is X shared among Y?" → X ÷ Y

(v) **Complex word phrases**:
- "What is 5 more than the double of 3?" → (3 × 2) + 5 = 11
- "What is twice 4 less than 15?" → 15 - (4 × 2) = 7
- "What is half of 10 plus 3?" → (10 ÷ 2) + 3 = 8

(vi) **Number combinations**:
- Single digits with single digits: "3 more than 7"
- Two digits with single digits: "5 less than 20"
- Larger numbers: "100 more than 50"
- Numbers as words: "four less than ten", "three more than seven"

(vii) **Answer format**: Single number (can be negative if subtraction results in negative)

**Difficulty mix**:
- 30% easy (simple "more than" or "less than" with small numbers)
- 40% medium (various operations, two-digit numbers)
- 20% hard (complex phrases, multiple operations, numbers as words)
- 10% edge cases (results in zero, negative numbers)

## Implementation Notes

### Data Sources Needed:
1. **Number ranges**: 1-10000 for various operations
2. **Objects list**: 100+ common objects (fruits, animals, toys, etc.)
3. **Semantic templates**: 10-20 templates per statement type
4. **Operation symbols**: +, -, ×, x, *, ÷, /

### Generation Strategies:
- **Statement 1**: Systematic (limited to 100 count targets × 12 templates = ~1,200 base combinations, need repetition with variation)
- **Statement 2**: Random selection with high capacity (number range × 20 templates)
- **Statement 3**: Random selection (objects × numbers × operations × templates)
- **Statement 4**: Random selection with balance across comparison types
- **Statement 5**: Random selection with order of operations validation
- **Statement 6**: Random selection with semantic diversity

### Validation Checks:
- All arithmetic answers are programmatically verified
- Division results are integers (clean division)
- Negative results are handled correctly
- Word-number conversions are accurate
- No duplicate queries

### Answer Format Rules:
- Single numbers: "4", "25", "-5"
- Counting sequences: "1, 2, 3, 4, 5" (comma-separated)
- Equal comparisons: "equal" or "both are equal"
